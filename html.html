• Jesse James Garrett's five elements of UX design: strategy, scope, structure, skeleton and surface. These layers are by no means standalone, and influence each other. Decisions taken at one plane can cascade up or down the layers.
      • strategy: user needs and product objectives
      • scope: features and content, requirements
      • structure: interaction design and information architecture
      • skeleton: information, interface and navigation design
      • surface: visual design
    • We briefly saw the different design activities and deliverables involved in the design process, including personas, storyboards, customer journey maps, brainstorming, content strategy, sitemaps, user flows, wireframes, prototypes, UX writing (microcopy), mockups, design systems, usability reports and analytics.
    • Since experience is abstract, we explored the qualitative factors of good user experience: usefulness, usability, findability, credibility, desirability, accessibility and value.
    • Becoming a UX designer is not a one-time activity, but a continuous process. In order to design user experiences, a UX designer must embrace the right mindsets: practice the craft of UX design, communicate brilliantly, embrace complex and wicked problems, think flexibly, and learn to learn.
